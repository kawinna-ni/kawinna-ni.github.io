I am a human-centered assistive robotics researcher and postdoctoral researcher at Kanazawa University. My work focuses on developing adaptable robotic technologies designed to support people in their activities of daily living and improve independence and quality of life. My research spans soft wearable robotics, assistive devices, humanoid-inspired systems, and bio-inspired robotic mechanisms, with an emphasis on safe, intuitive, and adaptable human interaction.

I received my Ph.D. in Engineering from Osaka University, where I studied musculoskeletal and humanoid-inspired robotic systems, including investigations of compliant joints and bio-inspired locomotion. My current research explores soft and wearable robotic technologies such as inflatable actuators, textile-based actuators, and variable stiffness mechanisms that can be integrated into everyday garments for assistive applications. Together, these experiences inform my approach to designing adaptable robotic systems for different users, environments, and physical needs.

Prior to academia, I worked in factory automation and smart manufacturing, developing IoT-based systems, machine vision inspection, and machine learning solutions using OpenCV. Combining industry experience with robotics research, my work aims to integrate intelligent sensing, adaptable mechanisms, human-centered design, and assistive robotic hardware to create practical technologies that help people in daily life.
