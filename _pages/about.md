I am a Postdoctoral Researcher at Kanazawa University working on soft wearable robotic systems for assistive dressing and upper-limb support. My research focuses on inflatable textile actuators and variable stiffness structures integrated into garments. I received my Ph.D. from Osaka University, where I worked on musculoskeletal robotics and bio-inspired locomotion. Prior to academia, I spent two years in industry working on factory automation, IoT-based smart manufacturing, machine vision, and machine learning using OpenCV. My goal is to develop practical technologies that help people in their activities of daily living and improve independence.
